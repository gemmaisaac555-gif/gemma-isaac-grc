# INTERNAL AI USE POLICY  
**Arken Systems Ltd**

*This is a thought exercise, not a real company.*

---

**Type:** Internal Policy – Artificial Intelligence Use  
**Owner:** Head of GRC  
**Approved by:** Managing Director  
**Version:** 1.0  
**Date:** March 2025  
**Classification:** Internal – All Staff  
**Review cycle:** Annually, or upon material change to AI tools, regulation, or Arken’s operating model  

---

## 1. Purpose

This policy sets out how artificial intelligence (AI) tools may be used by Arken Systems staff in the performance of their work. It defines:

- how AI tools are approved  
- where they can and cannot be used  
- how outputs must be reviewed  
- who is accountable for AI-supported work  

Arken Systems provides governance, risk, and compliance services to regulated clients. The standards we apply to client work also apply to the tools used to produce that work.

This policy exists because unmanaged AI use in a GRC consultancy is not just an operational risk — it is a failure of the governance standards we advise our clients to uphold.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to:

- all Arken Systems employees, contractors, and consultants  
- all AI tools used in connection with Arken Systems work (including personal accounts used for work purposes)  
- all internal and client-facing deliverables supported by AI tools  

This policy does **not** apply to AI systems operated by clients. Those are governed through client engagements.

---

## 3. What This Policy Covers

- Approved and restricted tools  
- Model approval process  
- Permitted and prohibited uses  
- Data handling requirements  
- Human oversight requirements  
- Disclosure obligations  
- Incident response  
- Staff responsibilities and training  

---

## 4. Principles

These principles guide behaviour where the policy does not give a direct rule.

**AI supports judgement — it does not replace it**  
AI can accelerate analysis, but it cannot take responsibility for it. Every deliverable represents Arken Systems’ professional judgement, regardless of how it was produced.

**Transparency is mandatory**  
Staff must be able to explain how any deliverable was created. If AI materially contributed, that must be disclosed. Concealment is treated as a conduct issue, not a technical one.

**Client data is not training data**  
Client information must never be entered into tools that allow provider-side model training. This applies regardless of perceived sensitivity.

**Governance begins at home**  
We do not apply lower standards internally than we recommend to clients. If we cannot govern our own use of AI, we cannot credibly advise others.

**Speed is not a justification for lower standards**  
If work cannot be properly reviewed, it should not be delivered. AI increases output speed — it does not reduce accountability.

## 5. Approved and Restricted Tools

### 5.1 Approved Tools

| Tool | Use Case | Restrictions |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Claude (Business tier) | Research, drafting, analysis | Internal use only. No client data. Business tier only |
| Microsoft Copilot (M365) | Drafting, summarisation | Internal + anonymised client data only. Must remain within tenant |
| GitHub Copilot | Code assistance | Internal tooling only. No client systems |

“Approved” means the tool has been assessed and is acceptable **for specific use cases only** — not unrestricted use.

---

### 5.2 Restricted Tools

The following require **explicit approval before use**:

- Any tool not listed above  
- Any use involving client data  
- Any use producing client deliverables without human review  
- Any use involving automated decision-making  

---

### 5.3 Prohibited Uses

The following are not permitted under any circumstances:

- Consumer AI tools for work purposes  
- Tools that use submitted data for training (where business data is involved)  
- Tools without appropriate UK/EEA data protections  
- AI-generated content representing real people without consent  
- Any use intended to mislead, fabricate, or deceive  

---

## 6. Model Approval Process

Any AI tool not listed in Section 5 must be approved before use.

### 6.1 Submission

The requesting staff member must submit an AI Tool Assessment Request including:

- tool name, provider, and version  
- intended use case and justification  
- data classification of inputs  
- links to terms of service and privacy policy  
- whether inputs may be used for model training (to the best of their knowledge)

---

### 6.2 Assessment

The Head of GRC will assess the tool against:

- data handling and privacy terms  
- security posture  
- transparency of documentation  
- suitability for the use case  
- regulatory and professional responsibility implications  

---

### 6.3 Outcome

A decision will normally be issued within 10 working days:

- **Approved**  
- **Approved for a specific use case only**  
- **Not approved**  

Approval does not mean low risk — it means the risk is understood and accepted within defined limits.

---

### 6.4 Review and Withdrawal

Approvals may be withdrawn if:

- terms of service change  
- data handling changes  
- new regulatory risks emerge  

---

## 7. Permitted and Prohibited Uses

### Permitted Uses (with approved tools and subject to Section 8)

- horizon scanning and research  
- drafting (with full human review)  
- text analysis and summarisation  
- internal tooling and templates  
- training and professional development  

---

### Prohibited Uses

- issuing client deliverables without full review  
- making risk or compliance decisions  
- entering client confidential or personal data  
- fabrication or misrepresentation  
- bypassing this policy  

---

## 8. Data Handling Requirements

### 8.1 Classification

- **Internal:** operational data (allowed)  
- **Anonymised client:** permitted only where fully anonymised  
- **Client confidential:** not permitted  
- **Personal data:** not permitted without DPO review  

---

### 8.2 Anonymisation Standard

All identifying elements must be removed or replaced:

- names → fictional or role-based  
- financials → ranges or fictional values  
- identifiers → removed or generalised  

If there is uncertainty, the default is **do not use AI**.

---

### 8.3 Retention

Outputs are retained under standard policy.  
Prompts and chat histories are not business records and should be cleared regularly.

---

## 9. Human Oversight Requirements

### 9.1 General Rule

All AI-assisted outputs must be reviewed by a qualified individual.

Responsibility cannot be delegated to a tool.

---

### 9.2 Non-Delegable Decisions

AI must not determine:

- risk acceptance  
- compliance sign-off  
- legal interpretation  
- incident escalation  
- hiring or disciplinary decisions  

---

### 9.3 Verification

All factual claims must be independently verified.

AI produces plausible but incorrect content.  
Failure to verify may constitute a quality failure or, in some cases, professional negligence.

---

## 10. Disclosure Obligations

### 10.1 Client Disclosure

Use of AI must not be misrepresented.  
Client instructions override this policy.

---

### 10.2 Internal Disclosure

Material AI contributions must be recorded in work product metadata.

---

### 10.3 What Disclosure Means

Disclosure does not reduce responsibility.

> If we cannot stand behind the output, it should not be delivered.

---

## 11. Incident Response

### 11.1 Incident Types

- data incidents  
- quality failures  
- disclosure failures  
- tool failures  
- policy breaches  

---

### 11.2 Reporting

All incidents must be reported within 24 hours.

Self-reporting is mitigating.  
Concealment is aggravating.

---

### 11.3 Response

The Head of GRC will determine:

- regulatory notification requirements  
- client notification  
- remediation actions  
- root cause and control improvements  

---

### 11.4 Client Notification

Where client impact exists, notification will be clear and prompt.

---

## 12. Responsibilities and Training

### All Staff

- follow this policy  
- use only approved tools  
- review all outputs  
- report incidents  
- complete training  

---

### Head of GRC

- maintain tool approvals  
- manage incidents  
- update policy  
- report to leadership  

---

### Managing Director

- approve policy  
- oversee governance resourcing  
- approve client notifications  

---

### Training

Training must cover:

- AI limitations  
- data handling  
- error detection  
- professional accountability  
- incident reporting  

Completion is required before tool use.

---

## 13. Compliance and Enforcement

| Severity | Response |
|--------|---------|
| Minor (self-reported, no impact) | Training / guidance |
| Moderate | Formal warning / tool restriction |
| Serious | Disciplinary action / client notification |
| Gross misconduct | Dismissal / potential professional referral |

This framework guides action but does not limit discretion where required.

---

## 14. Review and Governance

This policy will be reviewed:

- annually  
- after regulatory change  
- after tool changes  
- after incidents revealing control gaps
