ArkenScore v2.1 — automated lending decisions, retail credit division

Technical risk register
What this means for the business
1 Bias and discriminatory outcomes
Risk detail

The model is trained on historical lending data that reflects past approval patterns. Some of these patterns are likely to correlate with protected characteristics under the Equality Act 2010.

There is a particular risk around proxy variables such as postcode and employment type. These may be influencing outcomes in ways that create indirect discrimination, even though protected characteristics are not used explicitly.

No formal bias audit has been completed since the model was retrained in Q2. This means we do not currently have a clear view of how the model is performing across different customer groups.

FCA PRIN 12 Consumer Duty Inherent: Critical Residual: High
Board translation

There is a risk that customers are being declined credit, or offered less favourable terms, for reasons that do not reflect their actual creditworthiness.

From a regulatory perspective, we may not be able to demonstrate that outcomes are fair, as required under Consumer Duty. From a customer perspective, this raises a straightforward question about whether similar applicants are being treated consistently.

At present, we do not have enough evidence to show that the model is operating fairly across different groups.

2 Model drift
Risk detail

There are signs of statistical drift in the model inputs over the past 90 days. The Population Stability Index (PSI) is currently 0.28, which is above the 0.2 threshold typically used to indicate meaningful change.

The model was trained on pre-2022 data. Since then, economic conditions have shifted significantly. As a result, the relationships the model learned during training may no longer hold.

There is currently no automated trigger for retraining or recalibration when drift reaches this level.

EU AI Act Art. 9 PRA SS1/23 Inherent: High Residual: High
Board translation

The model is operating on assumptions that may no longer reflect current customer behaviour.

In practical terms, this creates two risks. We may be approving credit that carries higher risk than expected, or declining customers who would in fact be able to repay. Both outcomes have financial and conduct implications.

The issue here is less about a single failure point and more about gradual degradation in model performance over time.

3 Regulatory exposure
Risk detail

The ArkenScore system falls within the definition of a high-risk AI system under Annex III of the EU AI Act (credit scoring). This brings a number of obligations: conformity assessment prior to deployment, technical documentation and auditability, logging of decisions, defined human oversight mechanisms, and registration in the EU AI database.

Current position: logging is partial, no formal conformity assessment has been completed, and human oversight processes are not formally documented. UK expectations are also moving in a similar direction, although requirements are currently less prescriptive.

EU AI Act Annex III FCA SYSC Compliance gap: Significant
Board translation

We are likely to fall within scope of EU AI Act requirements for this system, and at present we would struggle to demonstrate full compliance.

The financial exposure is material, but the more immediate concern is supervisory scrutiny. If asked to explain how the model works, how decisions are made, and how risks are controlled, our current documentation would be incomplete.

At this stage, the issue is not only technical. It is whether governance around the system is sufficiently robust and clearly evidenced.

Recommended board decisions
01
Commission bias audit

Approve an independent review of ArkenScore against Equality Act and Consumer Duty expectations.

Output: clear view of outcome fairness across customer groups within 60 days.
02
Mandate model review

Request a joint plan from the CTO and Chief Risk Officer covering model refresh, retraining approach, and drift monitoring controls.

Output: proposed remediation and monitoring framework within 30 days.
03
Approve compliance programme

Fund a structured programme to address EU AI Act requirements, including conformity assessment and documentation.

Output: defined compliance roadmap and accountable executive ownership.
