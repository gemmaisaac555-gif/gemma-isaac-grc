Methodology

This section explains how the tool works and, just as importantly, what it does not do.

Scoring model

How the score is calculated

Each answer maps to a simple value: Yes = 1.0, Partial = 0.5, No = 0.0. N-A is excluded. Each domain produces its own percentage, and those percentages are then weighted before being combined into the final score.


Overall thresholds: 80+ = Low, 60+ = Medium, 40+ = High, under 40 = Critical.

Why use weights

Not every weakness carries the same risk

A flat average can hide what really matters. Weaknesses in access control, incident response, or privacy controls often matter more than weaknesses in lower-impact areas. The weighting here is a judgement call for this fictional scenario, shown openly so the logic can be challenged.

Why SIG Lite

Recognisable structure, adapted for the scenario

I used SIG Lite as a starting point because it gives the assessment a familiar third-party risk structure. The exact wording, weighting, and follow-up logic are adapted for this portfolio tool, so this should be read as a demonstration of thinking rather than a production-ready questionnaire.

Limitations

What this tool does not claim to do

  • It is a screening tool, not a substitute for real assurance or independent testing.
  • All answers are self-reported unless separately verified.
  • The weights are scenario-specific and should not be treated as universal defaults.
  • Compensating controls may exist but are not fully captured by a simple questionnaire.
  • The question set is fixed here for portfolio purposes.
