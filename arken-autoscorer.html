Scoring Model

Each question scores Yes=1.0, Partial=0.5, No=0.0. N/A responses are excluded from both numerator and denominator. Each domain produces a raw percentage, multiplied by its weight and summed to produce the overall weighted score.

Domain score = sum(responses) / answered x 100
Overall score = sum(domain score x weight)
Thresholds: 80+ Low | 60+ Medium | 40+ High | under 40 Critical
Domain Weight Rationale

Weights reflect Arken Systems as a defence technology supplier under Cyber Essentials Plus, NIST SP 800-171, and GDPR. A failure in IAM carries different consequences than a failure in Asset Management. Explicit weights make the scoring logic auditable and open to challenge.

Design Decisions
Why SIG Lite?
SIG Lite is a recognised industry standard adapted here to Arken context. Using an established framework rather than bespoke reflects how mature vendor risk programmes operate: the standard provides credibility; the weights and questions reflect specific context.
Why weighted domains?
A flat average treats IAM failure identically to Asset Management failure. For a defence supplier these are not equivalent risks. Explicit weights are auditable and open to challenge, which is a more useful governance conversation than an opaque aggregate.
Why follow-up questions?
A score shows where risk is concentrated. Follow-up questions guide the next conversation, framed for a governance or procurement discussion rather than restating what was answered poorly.
Why exclude N/A?
Treating N/A as failure penalises vendors whose scope does not include certain services. Excluding N/A from the denominator produces a fairer score while flagging scope assumptions that need validation.
Assumptions and Limitations
01Fixed question set. A production tool would use a configurable, version-controlled set updated for current threats and regulations.
02Domain weights calibrated for Arken context. Not appropriate defaults for other organisations without adjustment.
03Remediation flags are pre-authored for highest-risk IDs. A production tool would generate these dynamically across the full response set.
04Does not account for compensating controls. A vendor scoring poorly may have alternative mitigations not captured by the questionnaire.
05All scoring is self-reported. Supports initial screening only; does not replace independent assurance.
Governance Note

This tool is a portfolio thinking exercise. The scoring model, domain weights, question set, and output logic are documented here to demonstrate that design decisions in risk tooling should be transparent, auditable, and tied to specific regulatory context, not treated as black-box defaults. A vendor risk score is only as meaningful as the assumptions behind it.
