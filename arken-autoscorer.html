Tool 01 — Third-party risk

Vendor Risk Auto-Scorer

A first-pass vendor risk screen built around SIG Lite and adjusted for a fictional regulated-data scenario. It is not trying to replace a full assessment. The point is to show how I prioritise controls, where I push harder, and what I would want to test before I made a decision.

Working note

I weighted identity & access management, data protection, and incident response more heavily than some standard questionnaire models would.

That is deliberate. In this scenario, those are the areas most likely to turn an ordinary vendor weakness into immediate client harm.

Weighting

Why some areas are heavier than others

Not all gaps carry the same risk. I weighted identity & access management, data protection, and incident response more heavily because in this scenario those are the areas most likely to create immediate client impact if they fail.

Asset management still matters, but in a first-pass screen I would be less likely to stop onboarding over an imperfect asset inventory than over weak access control or vague breach notification.

I also did not want certification evidence to outweigh operational reality. A vendor can have a badge and still be poor at access reviews, incident handling, or subcontractor visibility.

So the weights are a judgement call, not a truth claim. In a payments-heavy or operational resilience scenario, I would probably rebalance them.

Scoring

Simple on purpose

Yes = 1.0, Partial = 0.5, No = 0.0. N/A is excluded from the denominator.

Each domain scores independently, then those domain scores are weighted into an overall figure. The model is intentionally simple. The aim is not perfect precision. It is to surface where the real problems are likely to sit.

Framework

SIG Lite gives it a recognisable shape

SIG Lite is used as a starting structure because it is familiar in third-party risk work. The questions, weights, and follow-up prompts here are adapted rather than copied.

Limits

What this does not do

  • It does not verify self-reported answers.
  • It does not capture every compensating control.
  • A single score can hide nuance.
  • In practice I would always follow this with evidence requests and discussion.
Fictional scenario. The aim is not to assess a real vendor, but to show how I think about third-party risk — what I prioritise, what I challenge, and how I turn questionnaire answers into next steps.
