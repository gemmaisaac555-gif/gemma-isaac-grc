# Board Brief — Third-Party Security Incident: Databridge Analytics Ltd

*This is a thinking exercise, not a real organisation.*

---

**To:** Board of Directors, Veriflow Payments Ltd  
**From:** Chief Risk Officer  
**Date:** 17 April 2025  
**Classification:** Board Confidential  
**Subject:** Post-Incident Review — Databridge Analytics Security Notification  
**Action required:** Note findings. Approve remediation investment. Decision required on Databridge relationship continuity.

---

## What Happened

On 3 April 2025, Databridge Analytics — a third-party supplier with access to Veriflow customer transaction data — notified us of a suspected security incident at 02:17. Our on-call engineer managed the initial response. Our CISO was reached at 06:30. We notified the ICO at 11:30, within the 72-hour legal requirement.

The incident itself was contained. No confirmed data breach occurred. The ICO notification was precautionary, reflecting uncertainty about whether data affected by the incident met the legal definition of personal data — uncertainty that exists because Databridge has not provided documentation of their data anonymisation methodology, despite a formal request made six weeks earlier.

---

## What the Incident Revealed

The incident response exposed three gaps that the board should understand directly.

**First: a known risk was not actioned.** In February 2025, our GRC function identified that Databridge's incident notification procedures did not meet the 72-hour legal requirement and recommended requiring a revised procedure within 21 days. That recommendation was not completed before this incident occurred. We responded to a breach notification from a supplier whose notification process we had already identified as inadequate and had not yet fixed. The controls gap analysis was correct. The follow-through was not.

**Second: our response depended on individuals rather than process.** For four hours, one engineer managed a potential regulatory incident alone, without adequate guidance and without reaching the primary escalation contacts. The incident was handled adequately because of the quality of the individuals involved. It should not have depended on that. An incident response capability that relies on the right people being reachable at 2am is not a capability — it is a hope.

**Third: we cannot currently verify a critical supplier claim.** Databridge's position is that the affected data was anonymised and therefore not personal data. We cannot confirm or contradict this because they have not provided the documentation we requested. We notified the ICO on a precautionary basis because we had no basis for confidence in the alternative. We are making regulatory decisions about our own obligations based on a supplier's unverifiable assertion.

---

## What We Are Asking the Board to Note and Decide

**Note:** The ICO notification has been made. The incident is under active review. No confirmed breach of personal data has been established. The ICO has acknowledged receipt and no further action has been required to date.

**Decide — Remediation investment:** The GRC function has identified seven remediation actions arising from this incident, including revised incident response procedures, tested escalation protocols, and a mandatory supplier documentation requirement. Estimated implementation cost: £18,000–£24,000 in staff time and external support over 90 days. The board is asked to approve this investment.

**Decide — Databridge relationship:** Databridge has not provided the anonymisation documentation requested in February 2025. They have not provided a revised incident notification procedure. They process data on behalf of approximately 40,000 Veriflow merchant customers. The board is asked to confirm whether to continue the relationship under the current contract pending remediation, issue a formal breach notice requiring documentation within 14 days, or initiate contract termination proceedings. Legal counsel has been engaged to advise on each option.

---

## The Underlying Issue

This incident is not primarily about Databridge. It is about a pattern this board should recognise: Veriflow identifies risks, documents them, and does not always complete the actions required to manage them. The February gap analysis was good work. The failure was in converting that work into completed actions with named owners and enforced deadlines.

The GRC function will present a revised risk action governance framework at the next board meeting — a proposal for how open risk recommendations are tracked, escalated, and reported to the board when they are not completed on time. The board cannot manage what it cannot see. This is a proposal to make open risk actions visible at board level before they become incidents.
