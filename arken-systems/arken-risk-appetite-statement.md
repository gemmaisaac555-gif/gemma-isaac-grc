## Purpose

This statement defines Arken Systems' appetite for risk across its principal risk categories. It is a governance document, not a compliance exercise. It exists to inform decisions — about what work we take on, how we operate, and what risks we accept, mitigate, or decline.

A risk appetite statement that sits in a folder and is not consulted when decisions are made is not a risk appetite statement. This document is referenced in client engagement decisions, operational choices, and the annual governance review. If a decision is inconsistent with the positions below, that inconsistency should be identified and either the decision or the statement should change.

This statement reflects the same principles that govern Arken Systems' client work: accepting risk honestly is more valuable than manufacturing comfort through optimistic framing.

## Risk Appetite by Category

---

### 1. Client Confidentiality and Data Protection

**Appetite: Zero tolerance for breach**

Arken Systems holds client confidential information — risk registers, incident details, strategic plans, personal data, and commercially sensitive material — in the course of every engagement. Breach of client confidentiality is not a risk we accept at any level. It is the single failure mode that would be immediately and permanently fatal to the business.

**What this means in practice:**
- Client data is never entered into AI tools without specific approval and appropriate anonymisation. This is enforced through the Internal AI Use Policy — not aspirationally, but as a documented, auditable requirement.
- Client information is stored only in approved systems within the Arken Systems Microsoft 365 tenancy.
- Engagement files are retained only for the period specified in the relevant client agreement and then securely deleted.
- Any actual or suspected breach of client confidentiality is reported to the Managing Director immediately and treated as a critical incident regardless of whether external notification is ultimately required.

**What we will not do:** Accept engagements that require us to handle data volumes or data types that exceed our current security capabilities. If a client engagement requires a level of data handling infrastructure we do not have, we decline the engagement rather than accepting risk we cannot manage.

### 2. Professional Quality and Analytical Accuracy

**Appetite: Low tolerance for unverified factual claims; moderate tolerance for analytical disagreement**

Arken Systems' value is the quality of our analysis. Factual errors in client deliverables — incorrect regulatory citations, inaccurate risk scores, misrepresented framework requirements — are a professional failure with direct consequences for clients who rely on our work.

We distinguish between two types of error:

**Factual errors** — incorrect citations, wrong article numbers, misrepresented regulatory positions — have zero tolerance. Every factual claim in a client deliverable must be verified against primary sources. AI-generated factual content is verified independently before inclusion in any deliverable. This is a quality requirement, not a guideline.

**Analytical disagreement** — a client disagreeing with our risk assessment, a regulator interpreting a framework differently, a peer reviewer reaching different conclusions from the same evidence — is acceptable and expected. Professional judgement involves genuine uncertainty. We document our reasoning, acknowledge our assumptions, and are clear about what evidence would change our assessment. We do not adjust analytical conclusions to produce the answer a client wants.

**What this means in practice:**
- Every client deliverable is reviewed by a qualified analyst before delivery. AI-assisted drafts are not delivered without human review and revision.
- We tell clients when we are uncertain. Manufactured confidence is a more dangerous product than documented uncertainty.
- If a client asks us to change an analytical conclusion without providing evidence that changes our assessment, we decline.

### 3. Regulatory and Legal Compliance

**Appetite: Zero tolerance for deliberate non-compliance; low tolerance for inadvertent non-compliance**

Arken Systems operates as a professional services firm advising clients on regulatory compliance. Non-compliance with the regulations that govern our own operations would be both a legal risk and a fundamental credibility failure.

**Key obligations:**
- UK GDPR and Data Protection Act 2018 — applicable to all personal data we process
- Employment law — applicable to all staff relationships
- Companies Act — applicable to our corporate obligations
- Professional indemnity insurance requirements — governing the scope and nature of advice we provide

We monitor regulatory developments in our operating environment. We do not advise clients on regulatory matters outside our competence. Where an engagement requires legal advice, we recommend appropriate legal counsel rather than providing it ourselves.

### 4. AI Tool Use

**Appetite: Moderate appetite for AI-assisted efficiency gains; zero tolerance for ungoverned AI use**

Arken Systems uses AI tools in the course of our work. We have a documented, enforced Internal AI Use Policy that governs which tools are approved, what data may be entered, what human oversight is required, and how AI-related incidents are handled.

We accept the efficiency and quality benefits that approved AI tools provide. We do not accept the risks that ungoverned AI use would create — client data exposure, unverified factual claims in deliverables, concealment of AI use from clients.

Our AI governance applies to ourselves before it applies to our clients. We do not advise clients on AI risk while operating ungoverned AI tools internally. That position is documented in the Company Charter and enforced through the AI Use Policy.

**Appetite boundary:** We will adopt new AI tools that pass the assessment process in the AI Use Policy. We will not use tools that fail that assessment, regardless of their productivity benefits. The assessment criteria — data handling, training data use, security posture, regulatory alignment — are not negotiable against convenience.

### 5. Operational and Financial Risk

**Appetite: Moderate**

As a small professional services firm, Arken Systems has limited financial resilience compared to larger organisations. We accept the inherent risks of operating as a small business — client concentration, revenue variability, key person dependency — and manage them through conservative financial practices rather than attempting to eliminate them.

**What we accept:**
- Revenue concentration in a small number of client relationships at any given time
- Key person dependency — the Managing Director and Head of GRC hold most client relationships
- Market risk — demand for GRC services varies with regulatory activity and economic conditions

**What we manage:**
- We maintain a minimum cash reserve equivalent to three months of operating costs
- We do not take on fixed-cost commitments that would be unsustainable at 60% of current revenue
- We carry professional indemnity insurance appropriate to the scope of advice we provide

**What we will not accept:**
- Engagements that require us to operate beyond our professional competence
- Client payment terms that create unsustainable cash flow risk
- Growth at the expense of quality — we expand capacity before taking on work that would compromise existing client service

---

### 6. Reputational Risk

**Appetite: Low**

Arken Systems is a small firm in a specialist field. Our reputation is our primary commercial asset. We protect it by doing good work, being honest about what we do not know, and declining work that would compromise our analytical independence.

**Specific positions:**
- We do not produce compliance documentation designed to create the appearance of governance without the substance. If a client wants a risk register that will satisfy an auditor without being used as a live governance document, we decline the engagement.
- We do not accept engagements where we have a conflict of interest that we cannot adequately disclose and manage.
- We do not adjust analytical conclusions under commercial pressure.

These positions have a cost — we will lose some engagements because of them. That cost is acceptable. The reputational cost of compromising them is not.

---

## What This Statement Is Not

This statement does not cover every risk Arken Systems faces. It covers the categories where our appetite positions are most material to the decisions we make. Risks not covered here are managed through operational judgement, consistent with the principles in the Risk Manifesto and the Company Charter.

This statement is reviewed annually. If operational experience reveals that a stated appetite is inconsistent with how we actually make decisions, the statement is updated — not the decisions retrospectively justified.

A risk appetite statement that describes aspirations rather than reality is not a governance document. It is a comfort blanket.
